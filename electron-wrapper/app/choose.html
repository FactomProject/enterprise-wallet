Choose your wallet type

Secure

Opening a secure wallet will encrypt all private keys and the seed. Keep in mind this means that if you forget your password, there is no recovery option. Be careful selecting this option

Not Secure

Opening a non-secure wallet does not use encryption, and therefore can always be opened. This does leave your private keys exposed in plaintext on your disk.

Secure

When using an encrypted wallet, be sure to immediately back up your seed upon first launch. This allows you to regenerate your wallet if you lose the password or the database gets corrupted.

This option will not convert a v1 wallet if found on disk. This is intentional, as converting any wallet to an encrypted wallet means the original wallet still exists, and still insecure. Addresses and seeds can still be imported/exported.

Not Secure

You are choosing the unsecured version of EnterpriseWallet. This means all private keys will be kept in plaintext on your disk. Use at your own risk.

Each option maintains a different wallet file. This means you can have both a non-secure, and secure wallet. To open either one, just reopen EnterpriseWallet and select the wallet you wish to open.
